About Me

I want to be good at what I do. Then I learned to never stop learning.

Well, first I'd like to say that I started programming around the age of 17. That was when I was in highschool and took an elective programming class. At the time I was not good and the teacher didn't expect me to even try...Well...until it was time to showcase our projects. At the time, we had only learned HTML and inline CSS. I believe her exact words were, "Wow Temicka!"

Well that was 12 years ago. Let's fast forward to today shall we? Ok, well... My niche for programming never went away, and I decided to learn front-end web development in 2014. Boy oh Boy, no one told me that Javascript was going to be such a challenge. Here is what I learned after I got my first front-end web development certificate from Skill Crush!

  • Mobile First Yup, started with those media queries!
  • Flex Box Wow it literally saved my life when it came to aligning divs and sections.
  • BootStrap Is it considered cheating if I use a bootstrap theme? I mean... come on, they save so much time!
  • Git and GitHub I can say that I have been utilizing that terminal now for almost four years!
  • Vanilla JavascriptI strongly believe, if you have a great foundation with Javascript, frameworks just come like a breeze. VueJS anyone?
